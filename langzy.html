Machine Translation at your fingertips

A zero-to-one product sought to bridge the gap, making Machine Translation services effortlessly accessible to organizations.
Overview

In 2018, the language industry was abuzz with discussions around Machine Translation (MT), a revolutionary yet divisive technology. The market was fragmented, and hurdles like high technical implementation costs, privacy concerns, and questions of translation quality limited its broad acceptance.

Our project set out to democratize MT services, making it easier for users to seamlessly translate content across diverse platforms.

My role

As the lead for product discovery and design, I collaborated closely with the Innovation Team's leaders and two external development agencies.

I was responsible for the entire design lifecycle, including research activities such as gathering and analyzing user feedback.

Opportunity

A niche for enhanced Machine Translation

Unmet needs in translation tools

The rising demand for Machine Translation (MT) services within memoQ's offerings highlighted a crucial gap. Feedback indicated that memoQ's complex workflows were a significant barrier to efficient MT service utilization.

Limitations of MT services

Our review showed that no MT services could translate richly formatted documents like Word or PDF while keeping the original styling intact. Furthermore, access to many of these services required API use, demanding custom development efforts from customers.

Speed, privacy, and cost

We gathered contacts from customers who had expressed interest in MT services. Interviews with these memoQ users provided valuable insights:

The primary use case for MT was translating materials for internal communication where quality was less critical.

Quick turnaround time emerged as the key benefit of using MT services compared to traditional translation workflows.

Privacy was a main concern when translating internal and sometimes confidential materials with MT.

Controlling costs was identified as a possible issue for utilizing MT services.

Opportunity
The market lacked a lightweight translation tool capable of integrating various MT services and processing richly formatted documents.
Discovery

Sparking rapid product discovery

Discovery in a few weeks

I facilitated a series of workshops inspired by Paulo Caroli's book, Lean Inception, to articulate the product's value proposition, define the ideal customer profile, map out a high-level user journey, and conceptualize a potential MVP.

Choosing the perfect name

Following intense brainstorming and debate, a team member proposed the ideal name, Langzy—melding Language and Lazy into a perfect descriptor for our product.

Solution

Google Translate on steroids

Follow the jobs

Based on the learnings from the interviews and outcomes of the workshop, I started formulating job stories for each critical job-to-be-done (JTBD). The designs were crafted to tackle the jobs defined in these statements.

Same same but different

“When translating content, I want to utilize the best MT service, so I can expedite message distribution.”

As Google Translate had become a commodity, I believed that adopting similar patterns would create a strong link between the user's expectations and the system's design. To align with Langzy's unique value, the familiar interface was enhanced with additional features.

The default state of the Translation page
The contextual MT service selector in action

Navigating MT service selection

“When uncertain about which MT service to choose, I want the most suitable one to be selected, so I can eliminate the need for manual trials.”

Acknowledging that not all MT services support every language, Langzy was engineered to auto-switch services for unsupported language pairs. We also refined our system to guide users in selecting the most effective engine for multi-service supported language pairs and introduced a preference setting feature. Consequently, Langzy selects the ideal service based on availability, user preferences, industry benchmarks, and cost.

Setting up MT service preference with drag-n-drop

Centralizing translation history

“When I look for a translation, I want to find them all in one place, so I can easily copy or download them.”

Given that customers would be charged for every translation made in Langzy, providing easy access to past translations became essential. Beyond this practical use, the History page also functioned as a log, allowing users to review their usage and translations over time.

Translation history with further options

Pricing

Crafting Langzy's pricing model presented a complex challenge. Initially leaning towards a usage-based model, feedback prompted us to adopt a hybrid approach. This model combines a fixed monthly subscription for basic platform access with a flexible, usage-based fee for translations. To address minor transaction costs and enhance user experience, we introduced:

  • A monthly plan covering the platform usage fee.
  • A top-up balance for pay-per-use translations.
  • Credits to streamline the interface and simplify transaction visibility.

Road to MVP

Shaping Langzy for public release

Learnings from Beta users

After the implementation of the initial designs, we ran a closed beta with selected users for several weeks. During this period, we actively collected feedback from our invited users. Their input primarily centered around three areas of concern.

Users found the performance of the translation process satisfactory. They expressed frustration over the manual effort required to submit content and retrieve translations.

Users indicated a preference for specific MT services for certain language pairs, leading to the inconvenience of manually switching services for each translation task.

Users struggled to predict their usage accurately, often leading to an empty balance and interruptions in their workflow.

Automatization for the win

“When I translate from Language A to Language B, I want to use the best MT engine, so I can share the translated content in better quality.”

Implementing custom integrations with various tools was not a feasible solution, especially for an early-stage product like Langzy. Therefore, we decided to enable our customers to create automation by leveraging Zapier's ecosystem. Zapier offers all the necessary features and tools for creating automated translation processes. Most importantly, our users were either already using it or were familiar with the tool.

One of our Zaps that used Langzy

Addressing usage variability

“When I translate from Language A to Language B, I want to select the best MT engine, so I can share the translated content in better quality.”

Our solution to the unpredictable and hectic usage patterns was the introduction of automatic top-ups, maintaining process continuity without manual intervention. To keep costs manageable, we implemented limits on automatic top-up amounts.

Configuring automatic top-ups

More customized experience

“When I receive content from the same source, I want to automate the translation process, so I can expedite the preparation and distribution of communications.”

Acknowledging the value of the global MT service preferences, we layered custom rules to allow for specific language pair and engine selections, enhancing flexibility and reducing manual setup.

Deeper customization of MT preferences for optimized translation

Public debut of Langzy

The insights from our Closed Beta were instrumental in refining Langzy. Although I moved on from the company afterward, discussions with the Innovation Team indicated their readiness and confidence to introduce Langzy to the global stage, marking a significant milestone in the project.

Rapid adoption and traction

Langzy swiftly garnered attention from Asian companies with US and European subsidiaries. Its integration with Zapier became a go-to automation tool for translating and distributing internal messages and support communications.

Put memoQ back on the map

Langzy sparked significant interest in the language industry, reinforcing memoQ's status as an innovator. This project demonstrated the company's commitment to pushing the boundaries of language technology.

The unforeseen challenge: COVID-19

The COVID-19 pandemic led to a strategic pivot for memoQ's leadership. In response to the changing business landscape, they focused on strengthening their core products and reducing expenses. Unfortunately, this shift resulted in the disbanding of the Innovation Team and the discontinuation of its projects, including Langzy.
