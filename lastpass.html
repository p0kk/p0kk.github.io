Enhanced digital security for you and your loved ones

Transforming digital security in LastPass, providing comprehensive protection and peace of mind for individuals and families online.
Overview

In 2019, LastPass was navigating through significant market shifts and rising competition that threatened its user base. Despite offering a popular free plan, the appeal of their paid offerings was waning due to newer, more innovative solutions from competitors.

Recognizing the urgency to revitalize their consumer product, the leadership initiated a strategic overhaul focusing on enhancing user experience and monetization strategies.

My role

As the lead designer, my main responsibilities were contributing to in-depth research, devising a UX strategy and concept, and ensuring continuous communication of our progress to the leadership.

My role was a fusion of research, strategic planning, and engaging with stakeholders, all directed toward catalyzing product growth and enhancing user involvement.

Challenges

Tectonic shifts in the market

Becoming a commodity

With Google and Apple introducing credential storage and autofill capabilities directly within their browsers and mobile operating systems, password management transitioned from a niche utility to a widespread commodity.

New competitors

The entry of new players into the password management market eroded LastPass's market dominance, with competitors outpacing in user experience, onboarding, and enhanced security features.

Monetization challenges

LastPass grappled with converting its vast free user base - 16 million users at that time - into a profitable segment, facing hurdles in effectively monetizing its services.

Insights

Unveiling digital security

Comprehensive research

To navigate the complexities of digital security, we conducted numerous studies to fully understand its various facets, focusing on how users perceive, expect, and address their digital safety concerns.

Essential insights

The research revealed valuable insights, leading to several company-wide initiatives. The following are key observations directly impacting digital security strategies.

Peace of mind: Someone is watching!

User feels secure when there is an additional layer of security or when they know someone is monitoring suspicious behaviour.

Invisible hand: Did I notice you?

User adapts to tools that seamlessly fit into their ecosystem and eventually become a part of their routine unknowingly.

Security meter: Blend of awareness & rigidness

The extent of user's awareness and strictness regarding their behaviour significantly influences their proactive measures.

Family manager: The evangelist of digital security

The Family manager is concerned about the password hygiene and online security of family members, especially kids and elders.

Evaluation of the existing solution

LastPass's credential review feature faced user dissatisfaction due to its access outside the main product, manual evaluation process, and limited focus on stored passwords, resulting in low engagement.

The old Security Challenge feature
Opportunity
Recovery from identity-related breaches is an attractive growth area as users are not very satisfied with existing solutions.
Concept

Enhancing security through Modular Design

Modular approach for clarity

Adopting a modular approach, I treated each data type as a distinct module to streamline interpretation, aligning with the law of the common region principle.

Comprehensive monitoring

Based on the uncovered user needs, I designed various monitoring solutions that would serve as an additional layer that helps them keep their credentials and sensitive data safe.

Password monitoring

The Security Challenge was reimagined, retaining its core functionalities but introducing a refreshed presentation.

Dark web monitoring

Focusing on email addresses and sensitive data, dark web monitoring empowers users with privacy-centric options to choose their monitoring scope.

Credit monitoring

Tailored for US customers, this module provides a real-time view of credit scores, adding an extra layer to personal security.

Proactive alerts

Incorporating alerts tied to monitoring features offers users a "set it and forget it" security measure, notifying them upon detecting issues.

Various proactive alerts

Unified Security Dashboard

While these features could exist separately, consolidating them into a single page provided a comprehensive view of their security posture, leading to the creation of this centralized hub: Security Dashboard. Also, this page would serve as a home for other security solutions in the future.

The initial concept of the Security Dashboard

Empowering family managers

Without a dedicated area for managing family accounts in the product, I designed an account selector on the dashboard for monitoring family members' accounts and enabled family managers to receive alerts about potential security concerns.

Account selector for family managers on Security Dashboard

Boosting feature adoption

In addition to standard marketing for new features, my strategy focused on seamlessly integrating promotions into users' existing workflows, ensuring minimal disruption.

Contextual prompts of Dark Web Monitoring
Validation & Refinement  

Adjusting to business viability

Reassessing monitoring capabilities

The evaluation of potential integration partners for monitoring services highlighted a need to rethink our feature set.

The comprehensive dark web monitoring feature wasn't financially viable for inclusion in both free and paid tiers due to high per-user costs.

Credit monitoring emerged as a costly, additional service, feasibly offered only to US customers, further complicating its integration.

There was a need to further simplify password monitoring and identify other settings to improve the Security Score.

Monitoring revised

After reviewing feedback and encountering certain constraints, we updated the monitoring solutions, which included removing Credit Monitoring.

Streamlined password monitoring

We streamlined the module by consolidating vulnerabilities into an "At-risk passwords" category, eliminating the unwanted insight into LastPass's standing. Additionally, we recognized the importance of incorporating other security-related settings into this module, such as trusted and permitted devices.

Reduced dark web monitoring

Considering the feature's business viability, we narrowed its focus. Dark web monitoring exclusively tracked email addresses stored in the user's Vault, continuing to prioritize user privacy.

The final design of the Security Dashboard

Omnichannel experience

With a finalized concept for the web Vault, I faced one remaining challenge: understanding its impact across the entire customer journey. Given LastPass's presence on various platforms, including mobile apps and browser extensions, it was crucial to ensure users received consistent safeguarding across all touch points.
