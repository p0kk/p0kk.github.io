Accelerating mobile app development

At Bitrise, we transformed the platform's core to streamline complex build workflows, significantly boosting developer efficiency.
Overview

Bitrise was one of the first Mobile DevOps platforms that provided CI/CD (Continuous Integration / Continuous Delivery) and DevOps tooling for mobile development teams.

In 2020, the build process management in Bitrise lacked the critical features that advanced mobile engineering teams needed to establish complex and efficient workflows.

Although Solution Engineers tried to meet these needs with workarounds and specialized solutions, the demand for an integrated, native solution clearly emerged.

My role

I joined the Build Experience Team, tasked with reimagining the developer experience of configuring and managing complex workflows in Bitrise. My role involved close collaboration with the UX Researcher, Product Manager, and Tech Lead.

I led the design strategy for the future of the build process, playing a critical role in driving the whole process, scoping an optimal feature set, and delivering a validated solution.

Problem Scape

Unlocking Pandora's box

Ready, steady, what?!

The project began with minimal guidance: a post from one of the founders on the company's public discussion board from 2017.

Revealing insights

The initial brief was outdated and vague, lacking in-depth insights into the existing issues. To tackle this, I collaborated with the UX researcher to start an internal discovery, aiming for a deeper understanding of the problem space.

We conducted interviews with stakeholders and advocates to uncover pain points and expectations, alongside gathering potential use cases.

We analyzed user feedback from various channels, such as public forums and Productboard, aiming to immerse ourselves in the users' perspectives.

We made a detailed competitor analysis, specifically targeting build process management practices.

Refining our focus

The discussions around Bitrise v2 prompted stakeholders to reveal countless pain points and use cases, leading us to narrow the project's scope. Considering the opportunities and their potential impact, we decided the project should address the limitations of the existing build model: improving support for complex workflows and parallel build runs. This decision gave rise to a new focus: Build Pipeline.

Insights

Faster the build, happier the engineer

User needs & pain points

From the outcomes of the research, we identified the main themes around users' pain points and expectations when it comes to managing complex build workflows.

Long Build Times

The absence of parallel build capabilities significantly extended build durations, preventing users from efficiently running tests and archiving processes simultaneously.

Hard to Debug Issues

The reliance on workarounds not only lengthened the debugging time but also obscured the identification of build failures due to inaccessible logs.

Difficulty in Scaling

As mobile apps expanded, the existing build model struggled to support scaling, resulting in bottlenecked build processes and workarounds.

Challenges in Full Process Oversight

The lack of clear visualization made overseeing and managing workflows cumbersome, complicating the access to generated artifacts and resources.

Business Problems

The company's struggle to provide a scalable, efficient solution for complex build processes not only led to lost enterprise deals and a competitive disadvantage but also placed additional workload on Support team due to rising requests caused by customized solutions.

Challenge
Deliver a solution that not only improves the efficiency and management of complex workflows for mobile development teams but also integrates smoothly with their existing processes.
The concept

Laying down the foundations

Minimizing disruption

Teamed up with the Tech Lead, we started designing a new Pipeline structure, prioritizing seamless integration with the current build model and the syntax of configuration files.

After several iterations, we developed a solution that kept existing workflows untouched by introducing "Stage" â€” a virtual layer bridging the new Pipeline with the existing Workflow layer. Workflows within a Stage would execute concurrently, simplifying the implementation of parallel builds without necessitating extra configuration steps.

Ensuring we are on the right track

To validate our new build model, we engaged Bitrise users in a concept validation exercise, having them diagram their complex workflows on a Miro board before and after introducing the new Pipeline structure. The positive results showed nearly all participants could craft more efficient pipelines within the new framework, reinforcing our decision to proceed.

Establishing principles

Based on the pain points and user needs uncovered, along with the validated system concept, I defined the following principles for the ideal user experience:

Seamless Update

Minimize disruption, enhancing rather than replacing existing processes for a seamless transition.

Transparent Overview

Simplify pipeline oversight through clear visualization, making it easier to grasp its current state and take necessary actions.

Streamlined Debugging

Emphasize solutions that rapidly identify and highlight pipeline issues, guiding users toward resolution.

Seamless Update

Ensure swift access to essential resources like app builds, test reports, and configurations generated during the build process.

One page to rule them all

Following the principles we defined for the new build model, I aimed to keep the existing information architecture intact by introducing the Pipeline Overview page. This page is envisioned as the central hub for all Pipeline runs, offering a comprehensive source for build-related information now and in the future.

Integrating the new page within the existing Information Architecture

Familiar pattern for engineers

Crafting the visualization in Kanban board style for its familiar and straightforward layout ensures that the Pipeline Overview provides a clear snapshot of ongoing processes without overwhelming users with information.

Keep it uncluttered

To maintain a decluttered space, the Pipeline Overview was designed to display meta information and prompts only when necessary, enhancing focus and information density.

Configuration-as-Code

Despite the vision for a comprehensive UI-based editor for Pipelines, practical constraints led us to reinforce our Configuration-as-Code (CaC) strategy, enabling users to manage Pipelines directly within the bitrise.yml code.
