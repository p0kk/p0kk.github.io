A better way to find the right influencer

Led a newly acquired startup in developing an enhanced influencer discovery experience and transforming data into actionable insights, empowering a growing customer base to find the perfect influencers.
Overview

Influence was a recently acquired startup by Brandwatch, aimed at addressing the gap in influencer marketing within their product offering.

While the acquisition was successful and cross-selling Influence showed promising results, new challenges emerged to meet the needs of Brandwatch's existing customers. Moreover, integrating Influence into the broader product suite introduced added complexity.

My role

I owned and led the user experience strategy, uncovering the problem space and exploring solutions in collaboration with Product and Engineering leadership, UX Research, and Data Science teams.

The vision and ongoing improvements I delivered were highly praised by the new customer base, streamlining sales processes and significantly contributing to closing deals more effectively.

Context

The new kid on the block

Paladin became Influence

In 2022, Paladin, a successful influencer marketing tool, was acquired by Brandwatch and rebranded as Brandwatch Influence. This acquisition introduced an influencer marketing solution to the Brandwatch Suite, filling a critical gap in their offerings.

The real pain killer: Discovering new influencers

While Influence provided a comprehensive solution for various aspects of influencer marketing, Brandwatch customers were particularly drawn to the Discover tool. It offered valuable insights and influencer search capabilities that were previously unattainable with manual efforts or workarounds.

The problem

New customer landscape

A gap between old and new customers

While our customer base had grown steadily, we realized that the new Brandwatch customers had entirely different needs compared to the pre-acquisition audience. The original customer base, primarily influencer agencies, had complex workflows and very specific requirements, which the product was reasonably well-aligned to meet.

In contrast, most new customers - primarily brands - were less experienced in influencer marketing, sought a broader spectrum of influencers, and prioritized simplicity over the complex yet powerful workflows favored by agencies.

Understanding new customers

Collaborating with the UX Researcher and Product Managers, we designed and conducted comprehensive research to better understand our new customer base. This effort aimed to identify their needs, pain points, and challenges with the current system.

Strategy

Where to put our bets

Instant value vs. long-term sustainability

After evaluating the research and findings, the Product-Design-Engineering triad identified key themes for Discover. Following several focused prioritization sessions, we defined the following decisions and strategies that shaped the upcoming roadmaps and focus areas.

Unclear and Overwhelming Insights

Make sense of data

Before — Lots of data, not much insight

User feedback revealed that interpreting key data was challenging, and the visualizations did not align with user expectations. As a quick fix, we published a detailed article in the Help Center. While this helped users, it fragmented the experience and added unnecessary workload.

Divide & conquer

To tackle this, my first priority was to revise how insights were presented to users. With a vast amount of data and multiple potential representations to consider, I adopted a pragmatic approach.

1.

Mapped the identified issues in the current presentation.

2.

Clustered insights (and their issues) based on familiarity, complexity, and purpose.

3.

Identified patterns to prioritize focus areas.

4.

Defined principles and guidelines tailored to each cluster.

Guiding principles

To inform the design and future development of insights, I established the following principles:

Clarity is king

Present information and insights in a way that is both accessible and immediately understandable to all users. If it takes too long for users to grasp the insight, we risk losing their engagement.

Design for decisions

Original datasets are like bags of Lego bricks. Our job is to construct something useful and valuable from those pieces, empowering users to make informed decisions.

Insight ≠ Chart

Avoid using charts and visualizations just for the sake of being visual. A simple number can often be far more impactful than a complex visualization.

Consistency builds trust

Use consistent formatting, terminology, and visual patterns throughout. Familiar structures allow users to focus on interpreting insights rather than deciphering the interface.

After — Guidelines and examples for insight presentation

Building on these principles, I developed a set of guidelines supported by real-world examples to ensure consistency across projects. These guidelines unified the team and established a framework for presenting insights effectively.

Provide explanations upfront for non-trivial and less commonly recognized metrics.
Combine related datasets to provide additional context and highlight spikes to draw the user's attention.
Do the math for the user. Break down complex datasets into meaningful aggregations, providing clearer paths to understanding insights.
Low Information Findability and Discoverability

Three levels of insights

Before — Lengthy process and overlooked insights

Product analytics revealed that users spent minimal time on the landing page. Flow analysis identified a recurring pattern: users frequently revisited the same pages in one session, suggesting they were attempting to piece together relevant insights within a section.

Qualitative findings supported this observation, with numerous questions and requests regarding "missing" insights. Surprisingly, a third of these inquiries pertained to insights that already existed, highlighting the inefficiency of the current content structure in supporting discoverability.

Scalable and flexible structure to deliver insights

To address these challenges, I applied the widely recognized Minto Pyramid Principle to restructure the content. This approach prioritized the most valuable insights upfront while enabling users to explore additional details as needed.

By adopting this strategy, I revised the overly flat information architecture to align it with users' workflows and mental models, creating a more intuitive experience. Additionally, the new structure introduced a scalable framework that accommodates the addition of new sections and insights in the future without requiring fundamental architectural changes.

Level 1 — Clear entry point with top insights

The new entry page serves as the gateway to the influencer's profile, offering a curated selection of key metrics and insights. This distilled overview allows users to make quick decisions by highlighting whether an influencer merits further evaluation.

Level 2 — Sections for related insights with improved information density

Insights are categorized into coherent, meaningful groups allowing users to efficiently absorb information related to specific areas of interest. The structure prioritizes the presentation of top findings from various datasets, ensuring that the information remains digestible and actionable.

Level 3 — Option to access full dataset for in-depth analysis

For users requiring a deeper dive, this level provides access to comprehensive datasets for each insight, empowering them to conduct in-depth analysis as needed.

Low activation rate

From good defaults to personalization

Before — Where should I start?

Product analytics revealed that first-time users rarely reached the activation event of viewing an influencer profile. Many users only realized the value of the Discover tool after several days and multiple failed attempts.

After — Striking the right balance in filtering

The filter and search experience is a critical component of the overall user journey. To achieve the right balance between simplicity and effectiveness, I collaborated with the UX Researcher to iterate on and test multiple design versions.

After — Good defaults & personalized experience

I aimed not only to improve the activation rate but also to enhance user engagement. By implementing a new UX strategy, I delivered value to existing users through tailored content and functionality designed to support every stage of the user journey.

These guidelines enabled me to take a more holistic approach to the user experience and focus on driving the desired metrics in the right direction.
